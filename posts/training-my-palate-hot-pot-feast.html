Training My Palate: A Pre-Departure Hot Pot Feast

Between the packing lists and the logistics spreadsheets, I needed a break. So we did Korean hot pot.

There's something about sitting around a boiling pot of broth that slows everything down — you can't really rush it. You add things in order, you wait, you adjust. It's basically the opposite of what our house feels like right now.

I've been thinking a lot about food since we got orders. Japan is going to be incredible on that front and I'm genuinely excited, but also a little nervous. Korean hot pot felt like a good warm-up — bold broth, fresh ingredients, communal eating. Good practice for what's coming.

The enoki mushrooms went in last. That's important. I learned that the hard way.

If you've made the move to Japan and have food recommendations for the Yokosuka area — please, I'm begging you, leave them in the comments.
